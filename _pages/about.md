I am **Peng Lan (兰鹏)**, a researcher working at the intersection of geotechnical engineering, slope stability, unsaturated soil seepage, uncertainty quantification, and physics-informed artificial intelligence.

My research focuses on physics-data hybrid modeling for geotechnical and hydraulic engineering problems, including rainfall-induced slope instability, Bayesian inversion of soil hydraulic parameters, intelligent assessment of high slopes and rockfill dams, and reliability analysis under spatial-temporal uncertainty.

## Research Interests

- Physics-informed neural networks and DeepONet models for geotechnical engineering
- Unsaturated infiltration, consolidation, and slope stability analysis
- Bayesian inversion and uncertainty quantification of soil hydraulic parameters
- Reliability assessment and risk evaluation of slopes, landslides, tunnels, and dams
- Intelligent monitoring, diagnosis, and digital-twin modeling for geotechnical infrastructure
